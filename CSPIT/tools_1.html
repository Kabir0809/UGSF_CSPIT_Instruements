10. UV Visible Spectrophotometer

UV Visible Spectrophotometer

UV-Visible Spectroscopy is based on the interaction between light and matter. When light passes through or is absorbed by a molecule, it can cause the molecule to vibrate. The wavelength of light most strongly absorbed by a molecule is known as the absorption maximum, providing critical information about the substance being analyzed.

  • Measure small volumes and precious samples accurately and reproducibly with a highly focused beam image.
  • Scan the entire wavelength range (190 to 1100 nm) in under three seconds, collecting data from single or multiple wavelengths at 80 data points per second.
  • Single Beam operation for precise measurements.

  • UV-Vis Spectroscopy is a quantitative technique used to measure the absorption of light by chemical substances.
  • Provides fast and efficient analysis, enabling researchers to obtain results within seconds.
  • Widely used in water and wastewater analysis.

11. Servo Shake Table

Servo Shake Table

Servo shake tables simulate real earthquake ground motions, allowing researchers to study the effects on structures and materials. The shake table consists of a Servo Actuator, connecting link, a Top table, and linear guideways. The Servo Actuator is controlled via computer, providing various base motions like sine, square, and random, resulting in corresponding vibrations of the top table. Linear guideways ensure the motion remains linear and accurate.

  • Type: Servo Electric
  • Maximum payload: 100 kg
  • Sliding table dimension: 500 mm x 500 mm
  • Frequency: 0-20 Hertz
  • Amplitude/Stroke: 0 to 1000 mm (±50mm)
  • Input Power: 230 Volts AC
  • Base Motion: Random, Sine, etc.
  • With Computer interface

  • Test the dynamic response of buildings, bridges, and other structures to assess seismic performance and improve design standards.
  • Evaluate components like dampers, bearings, and isolators to ensure they perform effectively during earthquakes.
  • Study soil-structure interaction, crucial for designing foundations and earth-retaining structures.
  • Simulate ground shaking to study soil liquefaction, a phenomenon where saturated soil loses strength during earthquakes.
